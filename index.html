Steve Jobs

charismatic, passionate, risk-taker, non-traditional, artist

Biography

Steven Paul Jobs was an American inventor, designer and entrepreneur who was the co-founder, chief executive and chairman of Apple Inc.

Apple's revolutionary products, which include the iPod, iPhone and iPad, are now seen as dictating the evolution of modern technology.

Jobs was an artist and designer who had no equal when it came to his attention to detail on his creations.

He was obsessed with detail, even detail that you could not see. His creative obsessiveness sometimes drove Job's fiercely loyal Apple co-workers crazy.

Contributions

Jobs was an innovative creator of user-friendly technology that continues to benefit us today.

In April 1976, Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniac and Ronald Wayne founded Apple Computer in Jobs’s garage.

In January 1984, Jobs introduced the first personal computer, Macintosh, later referred to as Mac that had a graphical user interface (GUI) and a mouse.

In February 1986, Jobs co-founded an animation studio called Pixar.

With Apple’s Mac Os X, an advancement of UNIX OS, Jobs retooled and modified his Mac computers using a stable, secure and powerful UNIX based environment.

Jobs and his team released the iPod in October 2001. The iPod along with the iTunes store changed the world's relationship with music.

The iPhone, introduced to the world on 29 June 2007 during Jobs's keynote at the Macworld Conference & Expo, globally made Jobs a household name.
